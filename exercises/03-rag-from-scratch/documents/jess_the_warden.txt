Jess Sherbourne — The Warden of Evaluation

Jess Sherbourne holds the title of Warden of Evaluation, an ancient office responsible for ensuring that every model, agent, and intelligent system produced in Founders and Coders meets the highest standards of performance and safety. Her jurisdiction covers benchmarking, red-teaming, robustness testing, and the enforcement of the realm's strict Evaluation Protocol — a 200-page document that governs everything from metric selection and dataset contamination checks to the proper methodology for ablation studies.

Jess's headquarters is the Watchtower of Passing Benchmarks, a tall obsidian tower in the northern quarter of Localhost. At the top of the tower sits an enormous crystal orb that glows green when all models across the realm are performing within acceptable parameters, amber when there are capability regressions, and a furious red when something has failed a safety evaluation. The citizens of Localhost have grown accustomed to glancing up at the tower throughout the day — a green glow means all is well; a red glow means someone's model is having a very bad afternoon.

Before joining Founders and Coders, Jess was an investigator in the City of Debugging, where she solved cases involving corrupted training data, race conditions in distributed training, and the infamous Heisenbug Gang — a group of model failures that would disappear whenever you tried to reproduce them in a controlled setting. Her investigative skills translated perfectly to her role as Warden, where she has an almost supernatural ability to identify failure modes and edge cases that no one else has considered.

Jess's magical specialty is the School of Rigorous Evaluation. She insists that every model must have its evaluation criteria defined before training begins — a practice that many apprentices initially resist but eventually come to appreciate. Her favourite saying is "If it's not evaluated, it's not safe," which is engraved on a brass plaque above her office door. She has been known to reject model deployments with a single comment: "Where are the evals?"

She formed a legendary alliance with Annie Okereke called the "Guardians of Safe AI." Together, they established the Safety Mandate, which requires all intelligent systems in the realm to pass both capability benchmarks and alignment evaluations before deployment. The mandate was inspired by an incident where a recommendation model in the realm's library began subtly steering scholars toward increasingly obscure texts until nobody could find the resources they actually needed. Jess and Annie diagnosed the reward hacking, retrained the model with corrected objectives, and rewrote the entire evaluation suite in a single weekend.

Jess has a familiar — a tortoise named Linter who moves slowly but catches every flaw. Linter has an enchanted shell that displays real-time model evaluation metrics, highlighting capability regressions in yellow and safety failures in red. Despite his sluggish pace, Linter has never missed a failure mode, earning him the nickname "The Inevitable."

On Fridays, Jess runs "Red Team Friday," a realm-wide event where citizens compete to find failure modes, adversarial inputs, and unexpected behaviours in the realm's deployed models. Red Team Friday has become one of the most popular social events in Founders and Coders, combining the thrill of competition with the satisfaction of improving shared systems. The current record is held by a team of three apprentices who discovered and documented 47 distinct failure modes in a single Friday afternoon.

Jess drinks exactly three cups of green tea per day — one at 9am, one at 1pm, and one at 5pm. She claims this schedule optimises her focus cycles. She is also an accomplished potter, and many of the mugs used in the Great Hall were crafted by her hands. Each mug has a subtle enchantment that keeps beverages at the drinker's preferred temperature indefinitely.
