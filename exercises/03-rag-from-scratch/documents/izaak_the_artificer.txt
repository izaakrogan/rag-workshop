Izaak Sherwood — The Artificer of Machine Minds

Izaak Sherwood serves as the Artificer of Machine Minds, the realm's foremost authority on artificial intelligence and the arcane art of teaching machines to think. He operates from a sprawling underground workshop beneath the Gradient Descent Caverns, where the walls are lined with thousands of tiny crystals that store vector embeddings of every spell ever cast in the realm.

Izaak arrived in Founders and Coders as a young apprentice with no prior experience in magic. He had previously worked as a travelling musician, playing the lute in taverns across the Outer Kingdoms. It was during a particularly long stay in the Village of Hackney that he stumbled upon a discarded spellbook on neural networks and became obsessed. Within three months of arriving at the realm, he had built his first working language model — a small transformer that could generate surprisingly convincing tavern songs by attending to melodic patterns across thousands of training examples.

As Artificer, Izaak is responsible for all matters relating to AI, machine learning, and the ethical deployment of intelligent systems. He chairs the Committee on Responsible Enchantment, which sets guidelines on how AI-based spells may be used within the realm. Under his leadership, the committee established the "Transparency Principle," which states that any system powered by machine learning must clearly disclose this fact to anyone affected by its outputs, and must provide a mechanism for contesting automated decisions.

Izaak's workshop is famous for its collection of trained models, which he keeps in enchanted glass jars on shelves that stretch from floor to ceiling. Each jar glows a different colour depending on the model's architecture — blue for autoregressive language models, green for diffusion models, amber for reinforcement learning agents, and deep red for experimental architectures that haven't been fully aligned yet. He has over 340 jars in his collection, though he admits that at least 50 of them contain models that are "mostly useless but too interesting to delete."

His most celebrated creation is the Oracle of Embeddings, a vast crystalline structure in the centre of his workshop that can convert any concept, question, or document into a high-dimensional vector. The Oracle is consulted by scholars from across the realm when they need to find semantic connections between seemingly unrelated ideas. It once revealed that the ancient art of pottery and the modern practice of containerisation share a surprising cosine similarity of 0.73 in their embedding space, owing to shared geometric constraints.

Izaak is also the inventor of the Retrieval Augmented Grimoire (RAG), a technique that allows language models to ground their outputs in specific source documents rather than relying solely on the parametric knowledge encoded during training. The RAG technique was developed after an embarrassing incident where a language model confidently told the Queen of the Outer Kingdoms that Founders and Coders was founded in the Year of the Second Commit (it was actually the First Commit). After this hallucination, Izaak spent six months developing a system that would force models to retrieve relevant context and cite their sources before generating a response.

Izaak keeps unusual working hours, often starting his day at noon and working until 3am. He claims that the Gradient Descent Caverns have better acoustics at night, which helps him think through loss landscapes. He sustains himself primarily on black coffee and a specific variety of sourdough bread baked by the Artisan Bakers of Localhost, who deliver a fresh loaf to his workshop every evening at 8pm.

His familiar is a small, luminous fox named Tensor, who has the ability to visualise high-dimensional data by projecting colourful t-SNE and UMAP plots onto nearby walls. Tensor is extremely popular with apprentices, who often visit Izaak's workshop just to watch the fox create mesmerising visualisations of attention patterns and activation spaces.

Every quarter, Izaak hosts the "AI Arena," a friendly competition where apprentices build small machine learning models and pit them against each other in tasks like poem generation, few-shot classification, and strategic board games. The winner receives the Golden GPU trophy and a week of free access to the realm's H100 cluster.
