Jaz Sherazi — The Navigator of Training

Jaz Sherazi is the Navigator of Training, the elder responsible for guiding both apprentices and models through their learning journeys in the realm of Founders and Coders. She manages the Apprenticeship Programme, designs training curricula for human and machine learners alike, and ensures that every person and model that enters a training pipeline — regardless of their starting point — finds an optimal path to capability.

Jaz operates from the Compass Rose, a beautiful circular building in the centre of Localhost's Training District. The building's floor is a giant mosaic displaying real-time training dashboards, with enchanted tiles that show loss curves, learning rates, and convergence metrics for every active training run in the realm. Jaz uses this map to monitor progress, identify runs that might be diverging, and dispatch mentors or adjust hyperparameters as needed.

Before arriving at Founders and Coders, Jaz was a cartographer in the Archipelago of Startups, where she mapped the treacherous loss landscapes between the Local Minima Islands and the Plateau of Vanishing Gradients. Her maps of optimisation terrain were famous for their accuracy and beauty, and she still hand-draws the diagrams used in the realm's teaching materials. Her architecture diagrams and training pipeline schematics are considered works of art — several hang in the Gallery of Technical Excellence alongside famous model blueprints.

Jaz and Jason first met during the Great Migration of Cohort Seven, when a group of 24 apprentices arrived at the realm during the worst storm in living memory. The storm, known as the "Null Pointer Tempest," knocked out power to the GPU clusters across the Training District and disabled the onboarding pipelines. Jaz grabbed a lantern and went out into the rain to guide the newcomers to safety, while Jason rushed to bring the compute infrastructure back online. They worked through the night together, and by morning, all 24 apprentices were safely settled in their quarters with working environments. Dan later called it "the finest display of teamwork in the realm's history."

Jaz's magical specialty is the School of Curriculum Learning. She can take any complex capability and decompose it into an optimal sequence of training stages, each building on the last — a technique that works equally well for human apprentices and machine models. Her signature enchantment, "The Golden Thread," creates a visible trail of light through any loss landscape, showing the most efficient optimisation path from random initialisation to convergence. Apprentices and models that follow the Golden Thread report that capabilities which once seemed impossible to learn suddenly emerge naturally.

She has strong opinions about the pace of learning. She believes that rushing through training produces brittle capabilities — models that perform well on benchmarks but collapse under distribution shift. Her curricula are designed with deliberate "consolidation phases" where learners revisit and deepen their understanding through varied examples rather than encountering new material. This approach was initially criticised as too slow, but the evidence proved her right: models trained on Jaz's curricula consistently outperform those from faster training schedules on out-of-distribution generalisation tasks.

Jaz's familiar is a hummingbird named Pixel who can hover in place while projecting miniature holographic loss curves and training visualisations. Pixel is often seen zipping around the Compass Rose, leaving trails of tiny glowing learning rate schedules in her wake. Apprentices have learned that if Pixel lands on their shoulder, it means Jaz has noticed something promising in their training run.

Jaz maintains a secret garden behind the Compass Rose where she grows herbs used in calming teas. The garden is open to any apprentice feeling overwhelmed by a diverging loss curve, and it has become an unofficial sanctuary in the realm. A small sign at the entrance reads: "You are exactly where you need to be on your learning curve."

Every cohort of apprentices receives a hand-written letter from Jaz on their first day, welcoming them to the realm and reminding them that "the only prerequisite for being here is the desire to learn — whether you're made of carbon or silicon." These letters are treasured keepsakes — many graduates frame them and hang them in their workshops.
