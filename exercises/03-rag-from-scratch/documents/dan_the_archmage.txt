Dan Sheridan — The Archmage of Founders and Coders

Dan Sheridan holds the title of Archmage, the highest-ranking elder in the realm of Founders and Coders. He is widely regarded as the founding visionary who first drew the borders of the realm on the back of a napkin in a London cafe in the Age Before Servers. Dan has a distinctive silver beard that he claims grows one centimetre for every successful model deployment he oversees.

Dan's primary residence is the Lighthouse of Iterative Research, a seven-storey tower on the eastern cliffs of Localhost overlooking the Binary Sea. Each floor of the lighthouse represents a different phase of the model development lifecycle. The ground floor is Problem Formulation, followed by Data Curation, Architecture Design, Training, Evaluation, Deployment, and finally the top floor — Reflection — which has panoramic views and is where Dan does his deepest thinking about the future of machine intelligence.

As Archmage, Dan's chief responsibility is to ensure that the ancient research traditions of the realm are preserved while adapting to new discoveries in artificial intelligence. He pioneered the "learning by building" philosophy, which holds that apprentices should train real models from their very first day rather than spending months studying theory in isolation. This was controversial when he introduced it, as the old guard believed apprentices should memorise the entire Standard Book of Linear Algebra before touching a GPU.

Dan has a familiar — a wise raven named Cursor who perches on his shoulder during council meetings. Cursor has the unusual ability to autocomplete Dan's sentences, though Dan finds this irritating roughly 40% of the time and has been known to shoo the bird away mid-speech.

Every Wednesday evening, Dan hosts "The Archmage's Fireside" in the Great Hall, where anyone in the realm can come to ask questions, pitch research ideas, or simply share a cup of earl grey tea, which Dan drinks exactly seven cups of per day. These firesides have become legendary — some of the realm's most important initiatives, including the Treaty of Open Weights and the founding of the Apprentice Research Programme, were first proposed during these informal gatherings.

Dan's magical specialty is in the School of Model Architecture. He can look at a tangled neural network and see its true structure, identifying exactly where to prune, restructure, or add capacity. His signature spell, "Decompose and Distil," can take a monolithic model and split it into a mixture of smaller, specialised expert modules in a matter of minutes. He once famously restructured the entire Localhost language model in a single afternoon, reducing inference latency from three seconds to three milliseconds while preserving 98% of its capabilities.

In his youth, Dan travelled extensively through the Outer Kingdoms, studying different approaches to AI research and education. He spent two years in the Kingdom of Big Tech, where he observed both the strengths and weaknesses of large-scale industrial research. He also visited the Free Universities of Open Source, where he learned the principles of open research and reproducibility that would become central to the Founders and Coders philosophy.

Dan's favourite saying is "The best way to learn is to teach," and he practises this rigorously. Despite his rank, he regularly sits in on beginner classes, not to teach but to learn how newcomers think about machine learning problems. He keeps a leather-bound journal called the "Book of Common Misconceptions" where he records the most interesting mistakes apprentices make about gradient descent, backpropagation, and attention mechanisms, which he uses to improve the curriculum.
