Landmarks and Locations of Founders and Coders

The Great Hall
The largest building in Localhost and the heart of the realm's social and research life. The Great Hall can seat 500 people and is used for ceremonies, conferences, keynote lectures, and the weekly Community Standup. Its most distinctive feature is the Ceiling of Contributions — an enchanted mosaic that adds a new tile for every meaningful research contribution made by a citizen of the realm. The ceiling currently contains over 40,000 tiles and is considered one of the great artistic wonders of the known world. Dan's tile is in the very centre, placed there at the founding, and it depicts a simple neural network: a single input node, a hidden layer, and an output node.

The Glass Terminal
The council's meeting chamber, located adjacent to the Great Hall. It is constructed entirely from transparent crystal, so that all proceedings are visible to the public — a physical manifestation of the realm's commitment to research transparency and open science. The acoustics are extraordinary; a whisper at one end can be heard clearly at the other. The morning Standup meets here every day at 9:45am, and any citizen may attend as an observer.

Port 3000
The main public square of Localhost, where all major roads converge. The name refers to the realm's founding legend, which states that the first spell ever cast in Founders and Coders was a web server that ran on port 3000. A fountain in the centre of the square perpetually serves responses to requests — visitors can toss in a coin (a query) and receive a fortune (a response). The fountain's responses are generated by one of Izaak's early language models and range from surprisingly profound to amusingly nonsensical, which Izaak says is "an honest representation of the current state of the art."

The Gradient Descent Caverns
A vast network of underground caves beneath Localhost that naturally slope downward in a pattern that mirrors mathematical optimisation. Izaak's workshop occupies the largest cavern, but the network extends for kilometres and contains numerous smaller chambers used for meditation, study, and experimental model training. The walls are covered in luminescent crystals that glow brighter as you descend, eventually reaching the "Global Minimum" — the deepest point of the caverns, where the crystals shine so brightly that no artificial light is needed. Apprentices from the Guild of Machine Minds make a pilgrimage to the Global Minimum as part of their training, though Izaak always warns them that in practice, finding the true global minimum is almost never possible.

The Binary Sea
The vast ocean to the east of the realm, so named because its waves follow a binary pattern — each wave is either exactly one metre or exactly two metres tall, never anything in between. The Binary Sea is home to the Semantic Kelp Forests and is a major shipping route connecting Founders and Coders to the Outer Kingdoms. The sea is navigable but tricky; experienced sailors from the realm have learned to read the wave patterns like a binary encoding to predict weather and currents. The main port is called the Harbour of Embeddings, which handles both commercial shipping and the tourist boats to the kelp forests.

The Cascading Style Sheets Mountains
The mountain range to the west of the realm, forming a natural boundary. The mountains are famous for their layered appearance — each geological layer has a different colour and texture, and higher layers override the appearance of lower ones when viewed from a distance (much like cascading style sheets in web magic). The highest peak, Mount Specificity, stands at 3,847 metres and is a popular hiking destination. The mountain paths are maintained by a volunteer group called the Responsive Rangers, who ensure the trails adapt to different weather conditions and accessibility needs.

The Overfitting Inn
A popular guesthouse in the eastern quarter of Localhost, known for its excessive attention to detail. Every room is uniquely themed and decorated to an almost absurd degree — the innkeeper, a jovial half-elf named Variance, has spent decades adding features until each room perfectly matches its last occupant's preferences. The downside is that rooms that perfectly suited one guest can be deeply uncomfortable for the next. Izaak uses the Inn as a teaching example for the dangers of overfitting: "It performs brilliantly on the training data, but generalises terribly."

The Null Garden
A mysterious empty space in the western quarter of Localhost. The Null Garden contains nothing — no plants, no buildings, no enchantments, no sound. It is a perfect void, approximately 30 metres square, surrounded by a low stone wall. Nobody knows how it came to exist, and all attempts to place objects within it have failed — anything brought inside simply vanishes, like tokens fed to a model with zero attention weights. Dan has declared it a protected site, arguing that "every system needs a null value." Apprentices visit it when they need absolute silence and focus. Annie once tried to interpret the garden's internal representations but gave up, saying "Some things are truly uninterpretable."

The Retrospective Rooftop
A rooftop garden atop the Lighthouse of Iterative Research (Dan's tower), open to all citizens. Every Friday afternoon, teams gather here for retrospectives — structured discussions about what went well, what didn't, and what to improve in their research and training runs. The garden is enchanted with a "Blame Shield" that makes it physically impossible to assign personal blame for failed experiments; the enchantment was cast by Dan himself after he observed that retrospectives became far more productive when people felt safe admitting that their hypothesis was wrong. Tea and biscuits are provided courtesy of Jaz's herb garden.

The Repository of All Knowledge
The realm's central library, a sprawling complex that contains both physical and digital records of every piece of research produced in Founders and Coders. The library is maintained by a team of archivists led by Chief Archivist Commit, who insists on preserving every version of every paper, model card, and training log, no matter how small the change. The library's catalogue system was designed using a custom embedding model trained by Izaak, and is considered a masterpiece of information retrieval. It is open 24 hours a day and is one of the few buildings in Localhost that is always busy, regardless of the hour.

The Pair Programming Pavilion
An open-air structure in the park district consisting of 50 paired workstations arranged in a circle. Each workstation has two seats, two wands, and a shared crystal display. The pavilion is where apprentices practise pair programming — a technique where two mages work on the same problem simultaneously, with one "driving" (writing the code) and the other "navigating" (reviewing and directing). Dan considers pair programming one of the most important practices in the realm and personally funded the pavilion's construction. It is in use virtually every daylight hour, and some of the realm's most important research breakthroughs have originated from conversations that started here.
