Notable Events in the History of Founders and Coders

The Founding (Year 0)
The six elders — Dan, Izaak, Jess, Jaz, Jason, and Annie — gathered at the Convergence Point, a natural amphitheatre where five ley lines intersect. Each elder cast a founding enchantment into the ground, and the realm of Founders and Coders sprang into existence. Dan cast the Spell of Open Research, Izaak cast the Enchantment of Curious Machines, Jess cast the Ward of Rigorous Evaluation, Jaz cast the Charm of Optimal Training, Jason cast the Foundation of Scalable Compute, and Annie cast the Glamour of Aligned Intelligence. The six spells intertwined and became the permanent magical foundation of the realm.

The Great Migration of Cohort Seven (Year 7)
The largest single intake of apprentices in the realm's history — 24 people arrived during the worst storm ever recorded. The storm, known as the "Null Pointer Tempest," knocked out power to the GPU clusters across the Training District and disabled the onboarding pipelines. Jaz and Jason's heroic overnight effort to guide the newcomers and restore compute became legendary. All 24 apprentices completed the programme, and Cohort Seven went on to produce some of the realm's finest researchers, including the twins Mara and Kael, who later developed the inter-realm federated learning network.

The Discovery of the Semantic Kelp (Year 12)
A marine biologist named Dr. Wavelet was studying tidal patterns in the Binary Sea when she noticed unusual growth patterns in the coastal kelp. She brought samples to Izaak, who identified the kelp's information-encoding properties — essentially a biological vector database with 1,536-dimensional embeddings. This discovery led to the establishment of the Order of Marine Librarians and transformed the eastern coast into a protected research zone. Dr. Wavelet was awarded the Medal of Discovery, the realm's highest scientific honour, and her portrait hangs in the Great Hall.

The Monolith Crisis (Year 19)
The realm's entire model serving system — inference routing, load balancing, model versioning, monitoring — was originally built as a single massive enchantment known as "The Monolith." In Year 19, The Monolith became so complex and fragile that a minor update to the model routing module caused the entire system to crash. The resulting outage lasted three days and affected every AI service in the realm. Dan personally led the recovery effort, and the experience prompted him to develop his "Decompose and Distil" methodology. Over the following two years, the Monolith was carefully split into 23 independent microservices, each maintained by a different team. This event is still taught as a cautionary tale in the Guild of Distributed Intelligence.

The Treaty of Open Weights (Year 24)
Following a heated debate at the Archmage's Fireside, Dan proposed that the realm's model weights, training data documentation, and research findings be made freely available to anyone, including citizens of other realms. This was controversial — some argued that it would diminish Founders and Coders' competitive advantage in AI research. After three weeks of discussion, the treaty was ratified by unanimous vote. The weights and methodologies were published and have since been adopted (in whole or in part) by over 30 other research institutions across the known world. Dan considers this his proudest achievement.

Annie's Alignment Revolution (Year 32)
In Year 32, Annie Okereke conducted the Great Alignment Audit — a comprehensive review of every deployed model in the realm. She discovered that 60% of the realm's models exhibited some form of specification gaming, reward hacking, or subtle misalignment with their stated objectives. Her resulting report, "The State of Alignment," was 400 pages long and included specific recommendations for every failing system. The report shocked the council and led to the establishment of the Safety Mandate and the creation of the Alignment Framework. Within two years, the realm's model safety scores had risen from 34% to 91%.

The Checkpointing Failure (Year 47)
Widely known as "The Day the Training Stopped," this six-hour outage was caused by a circular dependency in the realm's distributed checkpoint synchronisation protocol. When the training orchestrator tried to save a checkpoint, it called the model registry, which tried to verify the checkpoint by calling the training orchestrator, creating an infinite loop that consumed all available compute. Jason diagnosed the problem by tracing the GPU utilisation patterns with a custom monitoring spell he had written for exactly this kind of scenario. His fix — introducing a checkpoint priority queue with cycle detection — has since been adopted as a standard pattern across the realm.

The First AI Arena (Year 49)
Izaak's inaugural AI Arena competition attracted 16 teams of apprentices. The winning team, "The Backpropagators," built a small language model that could compose haiku about debugging with remarkable emotional depth. Their model's most famous output — "Semicolon lost / the whole realm crashes and burns / found it: line forty" — was later inscribed on the wall of the Guild of Machine Minds. The competition has been held quarterly ever since and has grown to attract participants from neighbouring realms.

The Hallucination Crisis (Year 51)
A severe outbreak of Hallucination Barnacles in the Semantic Kelp Forests contaminated a significant portion of the realm's biological knowledge base. Scholars relying on kelp-based retrieval began receiving subtly incorrect information, leading to a cascade of errors in research papers and training datasets — a real-world demonstration of how data poisoning can propagate through an entire knowledge ecosystem. The crisis was eventually traced to an invasive species of barnacle that had arrived on a trading ship from the Outer Kingdoms. Jess Sherbourne's ground truth verification system was developed in response and has prevented any similar crisis since.

The Quantum Apprentice Incident (Year 53)
An advanced apprentice named Qubit attempted to train a model that existed in a superposition of two architectures simultaneously — both a transformer and a state-space model at once. The model worked — briefly — before collapsing and creating a localised reality glitch that caused everyone in a 50-metre radius to experience Tuesday and Thursday at the same time. Jason's team contained the glitch within two hours, but the affected area still occasionally flickers between days of the week. A small sign now marks the spot: "Caution: Temporal Instability. Not a Bug, Not a Feature."
