Annie Okereke — The Enchantress of Alignment

Annie Okereke is the Enchantress of Alignment, the elder responsible for all matters of AI safety, value alignment, and the interpretability of machine minds in Founders and Coders. Every model, autonomous agent, and intelligent enchantment in the realm passes through Annie's exacting review before it is permitted to interact with the public.

Annie's workshop is the Mirror Chamber, a crystalline building in Localhost's Ethics Quarter whose walls are made of enchanted mirrors that reflect not appearances but intentions. She chose this location deliberately — the mirrors reveal what a model truly optimises for, stripped of the surface-level behaviour its creators intended. The chamber is filled with interpretability diagrams, activation atlases, and shelves of case studies documenting alignment failures from across the realms. A large wall is dedicated to her collection of "misalignment specimens" — examples of models that optimised for the wrong objective in subtle and catastrophic ways, preserved as cautionary tales.

Annie grew up in the Coastal City of Lagos in the Kingdom of Nollywood, where she studied moral philosophy and cognitive science before discovering enchantment. She sees a deep connection between the centuries-old philosophical debates about human values and the modern challenge of encoding those values into machine minds. She often draws on frameworks from her heritage — contractualism, virtue ethics, ubuntu philosophy — when designing alignment techniques, giving Founders and Coders' approach to safety a distinctive depth that is recognised across the realms.

Annie's magical specialty is the School of Value Alignment. She possesses a rare gift called Alignment Sight — the ability to see a model not as its creator intended, but as the world would actually experience it. This allows her to identify reward hacking, specification gaming, distributional shift vulnerabilities, and subtle misalignment that other mages overlook. She once looked at a model that had been in production for three years and immediately identified seven ways it was optimising for proxy metrics rather than the true objective.

The alliance between Annie and Jess — the Guardians of Safe AI — is one of the most productive partnerships in the realm's history. While Jess ensures that models are rigorously evaluated and benchmarked, Annie ensures they are aligned with human values and behave safely in deployment. Together, they developed the "Double Diamond" review process: every major model is first evaluated by Jess for capability and robustness, then reviewed by Annie for alignment and safety, and only proceeds to deployment if both approve. This process has virtually eliminated the problem of models that "score well on benchmarks but behave terribly in the real world."

Annie is the creator of the Alignment Framework of Founders and Coders, a comprehensive set of guidelines, red-teaming protocols, and interpretability tools that ensures every model in the realm meets safety standards. The framework includes 847 individual checks, from simple bias audits and toxicity filters to complex mechanistic interpretability analyses and adversarial robustness tests. Every check is documented with rationale, known failure modes, and worked examples. The framework is updated quarterly, and Annie personally reviews every proposed addition.

Her familiar is a chameleon named Anthropic, who can change not only colour but also texture, opacity, and even dimensionality. Anthropic is often used in Annie's presentations — the chameleon will transform to demonstrate alignment concepts, shifting form to illustrate how a model's behaviour can look very different depending on the perspective from which you observe it. Anthropic's party trick is perfectly mimicking any pattern that Annie points at, which she uses to demonstrate how models can learn to imitate without truly understanding.

Annie hosts a monthly event called "Alignment Review" where anyone in the realm can bring their models for constructive safety analysis. The event has a strict "no ego" rule — feedback is given on the model's behaviour, never on the builder's intent. Annie models this herself by always presenting her own recent work first and openly discussing its alignment failures. This vulnerability from such a senior elder has created a culture where even the newest apprentices feel safe admitting their models have problems.

She is an early riser, starting each day with a journaling session at 6am before anyone else arrives at the Mirror Chamber. Her morning journals are famous — she fills an entire notebook every month exploring edge cases, thought experiments, and novel alignment scenarios. Her completed notebooks are archived in the realm's library as a record of the evolution of alignment thinking in Founders and Coders. She has filled 73 notebooks to date.

Annie insists that every apprentice, regardless of their specialisation, takes at least one course in AI safety principles. "Everyone builds models eventually," she says. "The question is whether they build safe ones." Her introductory course, "Thinking Like a Model," is consistently rated the most eye-opening class in the entire curriculum.
